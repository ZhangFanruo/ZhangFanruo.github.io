LinearSpeech: Lightweight and Fast Text to Speech with Neural Architecture Search


Authors

  • Haozhe Zhang (Key Laboratory of Speech Acoustics & Content Understanding, Institute of Acoustics) zhanghaozhe@hccl.ioa.ac.cn
  • Ziyi Chen (Key Laboratory of Speech Acoustics & Content Understanding, Institute of Acoustics)
  • Zengqiang Shang (Key Laboratory of Speech Acoustics & Content Understanding, Institute of Acoustics)
  • Bolin Zhou (Key Laboratory of Speech Acoustics & Content Understanding, Institute of Acoustics)

Abstract

Non-autoregressive text to speech (TTS) models such as FastSpeech can synthesize speech significantly faster than previous autoregressive models with comparable quality. However, the memory and computational complexity O(N^2) of self-attention hinders FastSpeech from generating long sequences, where N is the length of mel-spectrograms. In this work, we propose LinearSpeech, an efficient parallel text-to-speech model with O(N) time and memory complexity. Firstly, We replace standard attention modules in the decoder of the model with linear attention modules to reduce the time and memory cost. Secondly, we add a novel positional encoding to standard and linear attention modules, which enable the model to learn relative order of input sequence and synthesizing long mel-spectrograms. Furthermore, we use reversible residual layers instead of the standard residuals, which reduce the memory consumption in training stage. In our experiments, LinearSpeech can generate much longer sequences than FastSpeech with much a lower memory and computational cost. Our subjective listening test also shows that the speech quality of LinearSpeech is comparable to FastSpeech.
